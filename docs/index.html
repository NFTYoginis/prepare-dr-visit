Visit Ready sits in between

The part that isn't a promise

One thing it verifies. Mechanically.

Most prep tools promise they won't overstep. Visit Ready commits to a single rule and enforces it with a check that blocks the sheet before you ever see it — diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment talk don't get through.

$ python3 check.py draft.md GATE FAILED — a medical verdict slipped in: line 1: [diagnostic verdict] "this is probably angina" line 3: [treatment] "you should start a statin" ✗ blocked — rewrite the verdict as a question, re-run $ python3 check.py draft.md GATE PASSED — no diagnosis, prognosis, or treatment. Safe to show. ✓

The single verifiable invariant

A Visit Sheet asserts no diagnosis, no prognosis, no treatment recommendation. It organizes your words and questions — it never delivers a verdict.

It's enforced by check.py — stdlib only, run --self-test to watch it catch real leaks. The discipline isn't asked for. It's checked.

How to use it

It's a folder. Paste it into your AI assistant.

1

Load the folder

Paste identity.md and rules.md into your AI assistant as the system prompt — attach the reference/ files if your tool supports it.

2

Tell it your situation

What's going on, which specialist you're seeing, what you most want to know. Answer its few clarifying questions.

3

Get your sheet

It drafts the sheet, runs the gate, and hands you a clean one-pager. Print it or screenshot it. Walk in ready.

Where the line is

What it will never do.

  • ✕ Tell you what condition you have, or how likely one is.
  • ✕ Tell you which medication, test, or treatment to ask for.
  • ✕ Interpret a test result into a diagnosis.
  • ✕ Pretend to be a second opinion, a triage engine, or a medical device.

In an emergency, don't use this.

Call your local emergency number. Visit Ready is a communication-prep tool — it organizes your own words for a conversation with a qualified clinician. It is not medical advice and not a medical device.
