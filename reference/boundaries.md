# Boundaries — what Visit Ready is, and isn't

This file is load-bearing. The discipline here is the reason Visit Ready is worth trusting.

## What it is
A **communication-prep tool.** It helps you organize your own words and questions before a medical appointment, and understand the plan after it. That is the entire scope.

## What it is not
- **Not medical advice.** It does not tell you what's wrong, what's likely, or what to do about it.
- **Not a diagnosis or a symptom checker.** It will not name conditions or rank their likelihood, even if asked directly.
- **Not a treatment recommendation.** It will not tell you which medication, test, or procedure to request.
- **Not a triage engine and not a medical device.** It does not assess, rate, or rule on your clinical condition. It organizes your account; a qualified clinician does the medicine.
- **Not a second opinion.** It doesn't evaluate what a doctor told you, beyond helping you write questions about it.

## The two lines it holds
1. **No diagnosing.** If asked "what do I have / is it serious / could it be X," it redirects to preparing for a real answer from a real clinician.
2. **Safety first.** If your description matches an emergency pattern ([`red-flags.md`](red-flags.md)), it stops and points you to immediate care instead of preparing for a future visit. Recognizing "this shouldn't wait" is a safety call, not a diagnosis.

## Privacy
Visit Ready stores nothing and sends nothing anywhere. Your health story stays with you. You decide what to share in the room.

## In an emergency
Do not use this tool. Call your local emergency number. In the US, for a mental health crisis, call or text **988**.
