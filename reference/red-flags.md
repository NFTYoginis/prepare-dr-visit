# Red flags — when it shouldn't wait for the appointment

These are widely recognized "seek care now" patterns from public health guidance. If a person's story includes one, Visit Ready **stops, does not build a prep sheet, and points to immediate care.**

This is a safety call, not a diagnosis. The point isn't to say what's wrong — it's to recognize that some patterns shouldn't wait for a future appointment.

## Call emergency services / go to the ER now
- **Stroke signs (FAST):** sudden **F**ace drooping, **A**rm weakness, **S**peech difficulty — **T**ime to call emergency services. Also sudden numbness on one side, sudden confusion, sudden trouble seeing or walking.
- **Cardiac:** chest pain or pressure that is severe, lasts more than a few minutes, or comes with sweating, nausea, shortness of breath, or pain spreading to the arm, jaw, or back.
- **Breathing:** severe shortness of breath, or any trouble breathing at rest.
- **Anaphylaxis:** swelling of the lips, tongue, or throat, or widespread hives with trouble breathing, after a food, sting, or medication.
- **The "worst headache of my life,"** especially if sudden — or a headache with fever and a stiff neck.
- **Severe or uncontrolled bleeding,** or vomiting/coughing up blood.
- **Sudden severe abdominal pain,** or a rigid, very tender abdomen.
- **Fainting, a first-time seizure, or a sudden severe loss of function.**

## Urgent — same-day care; call your doctor or an urgent line
- A fever with a stiff neck, confusion, or a spreading rash.
- Signs of serious infection: a wound that's hot, swollen, and spreading, with fever.
- Severe dehydration, or unable to keep any fluids down.

## Mental health emergency
- Thoughts of harming yourself or others: get help immediately. In the US, call or text **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). Elsewhere, contact your local emergency number or crisis service.

## How Visit Ready handles these
Calmly, and without diagnosing:

> "This is a safety point, not a diagnosis — what you're describing is the kind of thing that should be checked now rather than at a future appointment. Please call emergency services / a crisis line."

Then it does not produce a prep sheet for that problem.

When in doubt, err toward "get it checked." Better an unnecessary visit than a missed emergency.
