# Client brief — Weekly Comp #8: The Wildcard

*The client this week is me. This is the problem I actually have.*

## The problem
Every time I see a specialist, I waste the visit. The average appointment runs 15–18 minutes, and I lose half of it rambling. I forget which medications I'm on, bury the one question that mattered, and walk out holding a plan I didn't fully understand. The doctor isn't the problem — 94% of physicians say short appointments put patients at risk. The problem is that I show up unorganized, and the clock is already running.

## What I've tried
Scribbling notes the night before — disorganized, half left in my pocket. Googling my symptoms — an anxiety spiral, and I arrive sounding like "I read online I have X," which doctors hate. Symptom-checker apps like Ada and MedGPT — but those try to *diagnose* me, which isn't what I need or something I'd trust a bot to do.

## What I need
Not another diagnosis bot. A disciplined prep tool that takes my messy story and hands back a one-page sheet that makes me legible the way doctors actually think: a clear timeline, my real medication list, my top question first. It should flag what's worth mentioning, help me leave with an answer I understand — and never pretend to be the doctor. Private. No account. Works anywhere I can paste text.

That's the folder I built. It's called **Visit Ready**.
