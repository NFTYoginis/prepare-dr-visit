# Questions that get answers

Most people walk out wishing they'd asked something. These are high-leverage questions — they pull out the information that actually changes what you do next. Pick the few that fit; put the most important at the top of your sheet.

## To understand the problem
- "In plain words, what do you think is going on?"
- "What's the most likely cause, and what else could it be?"
- "What's the one test or step that would tell us the most right now?"

## To understand the plan
- "What are my options, and what do you recommend, and why?"
- "What happens if I wait, or do nothing for now?"
- "How will we know if this is working?"

## To stay safe until the next visit
- "What should make me call you sooner, or go to the ER?"
- "Are there side effects or warning signs I should watch for?"

## To make sure you actually understood (the teach-back)
End with this. It catches the misunderstandings that cause most of the trouble later:
- "Let me say the plan back to make sure I've got it: ____. Did I get that right?"

## A note on priority
You will not get to all of these. That's why the Visit Sheet ranks your questions. Decide before you walk in: if you only get an answer to **one** thing, which one is it? Put that first, and ask it first.
